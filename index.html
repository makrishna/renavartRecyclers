E-Waste

E-Waste, or Electronic Waste, is defined as “any appliance using electronic power supply which has reached its endof-life”. E-Waste includes a variety of electronic devices such as computers, mobile phones, televisions, and other household and office electronic appliances. These items can contain hazardous materials such as lead, mercury, and cadmium, which can harm human health and the environment if not disposed of properly.

Problem Statement

Electronic waste, or E-waste, is a globally growing problem due to urbanization and the sheer expansion of the IT and communication industries, and the lack of proper disposal and recycling methods. Further, it’s emerging as a major environmental issue (such as groundwater pollution & contamination, acidification of soil, and air pollution) and health (including serious illness, lung cancer, respiratory problems, and brain damage) concern due to its hazardous nature and increasing volume. The improper disposal of e-waste can result in toxic chemicals seeping into soil and groundwater, air pollution, and the release of greenhouse gases. In India, higher quantities of E-Waste are being handled by informal workers, and the people keep the majority of the obsolete electronics at home because they don’t know; how to properly dispose of them. Although the problem of this ever-increasing e-waste is complex, it provides great value as this E-waste is a rich source of precious metals that can be recovered and reincorporated into the manufacturing process.
